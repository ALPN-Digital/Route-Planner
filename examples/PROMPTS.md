# Example prompts

Once the skill is installed, just talk to Claude like a mate who happens to be a route nerd. It handles exact requests, vague ones, and slightly-wrong ones. Here is the range.

## Cycling

- "Plan me a 100 km scenic cycle loop from home on Saturday, and give me a few options."
- "Quietest possible 60 km road ride to the coast. I'll ride out and train back."
- "Gravel loop near the South Downs, about 3 hours, as much climbing as you can find."
- "Flattest 40 km from my place, I'm knackered today."
- "Plan me a ride to the best tacos on the west coast." (it will find the actual taco place)
- "Sunday adventure: I've got 4 hours and a bike, surprise me."

## Running

- "Scenic 15 km trail run near Box Hill."
- "Easy 5 km loop from home, roads only, no hills."
- "Half-marathon route along the seafront with one coffee stop."

## Hiking and walking

- "Best big-view day hike within 2 hours of London."
- "Flat riverside walk, about 8 km, somewhere pretty."

## Water sports

- "Paddle to the wind farm off West Worthing and back. Is it safe on Saturday?"
- "Calm sea-kayak loop near Falmouth this weekend, check the wind for me."
- "Open-water swim route along the bay, tell me about the waves and tides."

## Vague, lazy, or slightly wrong (all fine)

- "I want to ride 100 km, no idea where. You pick."
- "Something fun and hilly for Sunday morning."
- "Plan a ride to the wind farm off Brighton." (it will gently correct you: that's Worthing)

## Travelling

- "I'm in Lisbon next week, plan me a scenic morning run from the old town."
- "Quiet cycle anywhere nice in the Cotswolds, about 2 hours."

## Tuning it to you

- "Use my Strava to size it to my actual fitness."
- "Update my route preferences." (re-runs the short quiz)
- "Ignore my profile for this one, just give me the fastest line."
- "Forget the coffee stops, I just want to ride."

## What you get back

A GPX file to load onto your device, your estimated time based on your Strava pace, the weather for the day (wind and waves too, for water), any stops you confirmed, and a short brief on what makes the route good and what to watch for.
